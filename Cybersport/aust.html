Our Work

Astralis is a professional Counter Strike: Global Offensive esports team, founded in January 2016 by Team Question Mark members after they left Team SoloMid. They received investments of several million DKK from Sunstone Capital and Danish entrepreneur Tommy Ahlers and include Astralis ApS. The organization is managed by RFRSH Entertainment, and the players own an undisclosed amount of capital. When the founding member of Rene 'cajunb' Borg was transferred to the Dignitas team on May 19, 2016, the team announced that it would retain a small portion of its shares in the company, but would no longer play an active role in its daily activities, on October 19, 2016 it was announced that Astralis sold Finn 'Karrigan' Andersen to FaZe Clan, and on 24 October it was announced that Lukas 'gla1ve' Rossander had joined Astralis for an undisclosed fee.

Win 1

Tournament

Danish Astralis defeated Team Liquid in the final match of ELEAGUE CS: GO Premier 2018 with a score of 2: 0 (16: 5 @ Inferno, 16:12 @ Nuke). Triumphant tournament earned 500 thousand dollars from a total prize fund of $ 1 million. Losing finalist received 200 thousand.

ELEAGUE CS: GO Premier 2018 took place in Atlanta. Eight teams took part in the competition. Recall Ukrainian Natus Vincere reached the semifinals.

Win 2

Tournament

In the final of DreamHack Open Winter 2017, the team from the CIS Natus Vincere won the European squad with a score of 2-0 (de_cbble 16-4 and de_inferno 16-11).

Astralis produced their first victory in the major and won the largest amount during its existence - $ 500 thousand.

Win 3

Tournament

The Astralis Danish top five won the Esports Championship Series championship cup after defeating Team Liquid in the grand final match.

The final match of the tournament began with Mirage. Astralis won all the key rounds for a successful start, which subsequently helped her earn a huge advantage - 9-0. Then TL earned the first three points, the last of which went to her thanks to the 1v3-clutch from Twistzz - 9-3. At the end of the half, Liquid had the opportunity to close the gap to double, but it lost to the “force-buy” of the Danes, which led to a mark of 11-4
