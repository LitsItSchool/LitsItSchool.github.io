Ford GT 40

"Ford GT 40". This is a sports car designed to win at the "Ferrari" at long distances. The car was called GT (short for Gran Turismo), and added 40, which showed his full height of 40 dime, as required by the rules. The price of this drag-car for 2005 was $ 150,000. Their production began in the distant 1965 in which the founder of Ford, Henry Ford wanted his new car to participate in Le-Mane. It was specially designed for wins in long distance races against the "Ferrari" (who won Le Mans six times in a row from 1960 to 1965). GT40 P 1075 was the first car to win in Le Mans twice (1968 and 1969); This car used the Gurney-Weslake engine of a special alloy. Used engines of Ford V8 large volume (4.7 liters and 7 liters), compared with the Ferrari V12, 3.0 liters or 4.0 liters.

Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren

Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren. This supercar, produced jointly by the German company Mercedes-Benz and the British manufacturer McLaren Automotive from 2003 to 2009. It is an acronym and stands for "Sport Leicht Rennsport" (with it "sporty lightweight racing"). The body of this machine is made entirely of carbon fiber, the only part of the body made of aluminum is the motor frame. The price of a supercar since the sale has tripled. All these machines were used in many films, for example, in the film "Fast and Furious". In the film, they are used at every step, as the characters of the film do not save on their cars, although the price of these "crumbs" is considerable.
